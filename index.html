Photo Guide

📐
Three Essential Poses

Front: Stand tall, feet hip-width apart, arms relaxed at sides. Shoulders back, looking straight ahead. This shows core definition, symmetry, and overall body fat distribution.

Side: Turn 90° to your left. Arms relaxed. This reveals posture changes, belly fat reduction, and muscle growth in arms, glutes and hamstrings.

Back: Face away from camera. Arms relaxed at sides. This tracks back muscle definition, shoulder width, and glute development.

🔆
Lighting

Use natural light from a large window. Stand 6-10 feet from the window with light falling evenly on your body. Avoid overhead lighting which creates misleading shadows. The best times are morning (8-10 AM) or late afternoon.

📷
Camera Setup

Place your phone at chest height on a stable surface or tripod. Use the timer function or this app's camera. Mark the phone's position with tape so you can replicate it exactly each week. Keep the same distance every time.

🔄
Consistency Rules

Take photos at the same time each week — ideally first thing in the morning before eating. Wear the same minimal clothing (shorts for men, sports bra + shorts for women). Use the same location with a plain background. The ghost overlay will help you match your position exactly.

⏱️
When to Photograph

Weekly is ideal for staying motivated while seeing real change. Photograph fasted in the morning for the most consistent baseline. Avoid photographing after workouts (temporary pump), large meals (bloating), or when dehydrated (false leanness).

🧠
Mindset

Treat photos as data, not judgment. Progress isn't linear — some weeks you'll see big changes, others won't look different. Trust the process. The side-by-side comparison of Week 1 vs Now is where the magic happens.
